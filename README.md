Active listening, clarification, and empathy are cognitive processes through which the requirements of the users are processed. Users often think of problems in terms of their end goals instead of the technical requirements they are supposed to present, so my job as a developer is to find a way to convert goals into features that work. The tool of creating **user stories**, based on the user's perspective:*who* the user is, *what* they want to do, and *why* it is important, really helps in bridging these two aspects. This layout of the specifications service reduces the risk of developing code with no added value. User stories are also a means of continuous feedback by virtue of which the requirements are summoned to change as users start to understand their own needs.

In my programming work, I prefer going the route of doing things in Iteration rather than trying to design everything up front. The Agile development framework promotes this way of thinking by rebuilding the product in small increments and refocusing on relevant steps regularly. In my future work, I will try to embed Agile practices such as sprint planning, daily stand-ups, backlog refinement, and retrospectives. These processes will help flexibility, diminish the risk and simplify the respond to project changes. On top of this, Agile emphasizes collaboration and transparency, the essence of which is making sure each member of the team sees both the progress and the obstacles faced.

Being a good team member in the field of software development transcends writing clean code. It is about clear communication, being accountable, and showing respect for the right to think differently. A true team player turns to be receptive to the feedback, renders help to other team members and is focused on the common goal rather than on an individual achievement. Consistency is fundamental; keep your word and let them know in advance if any issues arise. In the end, good software development is a group commitment and it is built on trust, cooperation, and the common goal of developing the solutions that users genuinely want.
